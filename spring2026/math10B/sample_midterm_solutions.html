Question 1

Compute the double integral:

\[ \int_{0}^{1}\int_{y}^{1} e^{y/x} \, dx \, dy .\]

Solution:

The inner integral \(\int e^{y/x} \, dx\) is not elementary. We must change the order of integration. The current region is \(0 \le y \le 1\) and \(y \le x \le 1\). This is a triangle with vertices \((0,0), (1,0), (1,1)\).

Changing the order to \(dy \, dx\), the bounds become \(0 \le x \le 1\) and \(0 \le y \le x\):

\[ \int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{x} e^{y/x} \, dy \, dx \]

Evaluate the inner integral:

\[ \int_{0}^{x} e^{y/x} \, dy = \left[ x e^{y/x} \right]_{y=0}^{y=x} = x(e^1 - e^0) = x(e-1) \]

Now evaluate the outer integral:

\[ \int_{0}^{1} x(e-1) \, dx = (e-1) \left[ \frac{1}{2}x^2 \right]_{0}^{1} = \frac{e-1}{2}. \]

Question 2

Set up a triple integral to compute the volume enclosed between the surfaces \(x=0\), \(y=0\), \(x+2y=6\), \(z=0\), and \(x+y+z=0\). Evaluate this integral.

Solution:

Step 1: Determine the bounds.

The region in the \(xy\)-plane is bounded by \(x=0\), \(y=0\), and the line \(x+2y=6\). Solving for \(y\), we get \(y = 3 - \frac{x}{2}\). The \(x\)-intercept of this line is \(x=6\).

For the vertical bounds, \(z\) ranges from the plane \(z=0\) to the plane \(z = -x - y\). Note that in the first quadrant where \(x, y \ge 0\), the plane \(z = -x - y\) lies below the \(xy\)-plane.

Step 2: Set up the integral.

\[ V = \int_{0}^{6} \int_{0}^{3 - \frac{x}{2}} \int_{-x-y}^0 \, dz \, dy \, dx \]

Step 3: Evaluate the integral.

Evaluate the inner integral with respect to \(z\):

\[ \int_{0}^{x+y} dz = [z]_{-x-y}^{0} = x+y \]

Evaluate the middle integral with respect to \(y\):

\[ \int_{0}^{3 - \frac{x}{2}} (x+y) \, dy = \left[ xy + \frac{1}{2}y^2 \right]_{0}^{3 - \frac{x}{2}} \]

\[ = x\left(3 - \frac{x}{2}\right) + \frac{1}{2}\left(3 - \frac{x}{2}\right)^2 \]

\[ = 3x - \frac{x^2}{2} + \frac{1}{2}\left(9 - 3x + \frac{x^2}{4}\right) \]

\[ = 3x - \frac{x^2}{2} + \frac{9}{2} - \frac{3x}{2} + \frac{x^2}{8} = \frac{3x}{2} - \frac{3x^2}{8} + \frac{9}{2} \]

Evaluate the outer integral with respect to \(x\):

\[ \int_{0}^{6} \left( \frac{3x}{2} - \frac{3x^2}{8} + \frac{9}{2} \right) \, dx \]

\[ = \left[ \frac{3x^2}{4} - \frac{x^3}{8} + \frac{9x}{2} \right]_{0}^{6} \]

\[ = \frac{3(36)}{4} - \frac{216}{8} + \frac{9(6)}{2} \]

\[ = 27 - 27 + 27 = 27.\]

Question 3

Find the volume of the solid enclosed by the surfaces \(x^2 + y^2 = \frac{1}{5}z^2\) and \(z = 5 + \sqrt{5 - x^2 - y^2}\) above the \(xy\)-plane.

Solution:

Using cylindrical coordinates: \(r^2 = x^2 + y^2\). The surfaces are \(z = \sqrt{5}r\) and \(z = 5 + \sqrt{5 - r^2}\).

Find the intersection: \(\sqrt{5}r = 5 + \sqrt{5 - r^2}\). Hence, \(\sqrt{5}r -5 = \sqrt{5 - r^2}\). Squaring both sides, we get \(5r^2+25 - 10\sqrt{5}r = 5-r^2\), which implies that \(6r^2-10\sqrt{5}r+20=0\). Solving for \(r\) shows that \(r = \sqrt{5}\) or \(r = 2\sqrt{5}/3\). However, the latter value of \(r\) doesn't satisfy the equation, so we can ignore it.

The volume is the integral over the disk of radius \(\sqrt{5}\):

\[ V = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\sqrt{5}} \int_{\sqrt{5}r}^{5 + \sqrt{5-r^2}} r \, dz \, dr \, d\theta \]

\[ V = 2\pi \int_{0}^{\sqrt{5}} (5r + r\sqrt{5-r^2} - \sqrt{5}r^2) \, dr \]

Evaluating this yields: \[ V = 2\pi \left[ \frac{5}{2}r^2 - \frac{1}{3}(5-r^2)^{3/2} - \frac{\sqrt{5}}{3}r^3 \right]_{0}^{\sqrt{5}} = 2\pi \left( \frac{25}{2} + \frac{5\sqrt{5}}{3} - \frac{5\sqrt{5}}{3} \right) = \frac{25\pi}{3}.\]

Question 4

Evaluate the integral: \[ \iint_{D} xy \, dA \]

where \(D\) is the region in the first quadrant bounded by \(x^2 + y^2 = 4, x^2 + y^2 = 9, x^2 - y^2 = 1, x^2 - y^2 = 4\).

Solution:

Step 1: Define the transformation.

Let \(u = x^2 + y^2\) and \(v = x^2 - y^2\). The bounds for the region in the \(uv\)-plane are \(4 \le u \le 9\) and \(1 \le v \le 4\).

To compute the Jacobian directly, we first solve for \(x\) and \(y\) in terms of \(u\) and \(v\):

Adding the equations: \(2x^2 = u + v \implies x = \sqrt{\frac{u+v}{2}}\)

Subtracting the equations: \(2y^2 = u - v \implies y = \sqrt{\frac{u-v}{2}}\)

Step 2: Compute partial derivatives.

\[ \frac{\partial x}{\partial u} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{u+v}{2} \right)^{-1/2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4x} \]

\[ \frac{\partial x}{\partial v} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{u+v}{2} \right)^{-1/2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4x} \]

\[ \frac{\partial y}{\partial u} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{u-v}{2} \right)^{-1/2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4y} \]

\[ \frac{\partial y}{\partial v} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{u-v}{2} \right)^{-1/2} \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) = -\frac{1}{4y} \]

Step 3: Compute the Jacobian determinant \( J = \frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)} \).

\[ J = \begin{vmatrix} \frac{\partial x}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial x}{\partial v} \\ \frac{\partial y}{\partial u} & \frac{\partial y}{\partial v} \end{vmatrix} = \begin{vmatrix} \frac{1}{4x} & \frac{1}{4x} \\ \frac{1}{4y} & -\frac{1}{4y} \end{vmatrix} \]

\[ J = \left( \frac{1}{4x} \right) \left( -\frac{1}{4y} \right) - \left( \frac{1}{4x} \right) \left( \frac{1}{4y} \right) = -\frac{1}{16xy} - \frac{1}{16xy} = -\frac{2}{16xy} = -\frac{1}{8xy} \]

The absolute value is \( |J| = \frac{1}{8xy} \).

Step 4: Evaluate the integral.

\[ \iint_{D} xy \, dA = \int_{1}^{4} \int_{4}^{9} xy \cdot \left| \frac{1}{8xy} \right| \, du \, dv \]

\[ = \frac{1}{8} \int_{1}^{4} \int_{4}^{9} 1 \, du \, dv = \frac{1}{8} [u]_{4}^{9} \cdot [v]_{1}^{4} \]

\[ = \frac{1}{8} (5)(3) = \frac{15}{8}. \]
