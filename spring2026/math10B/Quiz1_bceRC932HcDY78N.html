Math 10B - Quiz 1: Double Integrals

Problem 1

(4 points) Evaluate the double integral:

\[\iint_{R}(x^{2}y+xy+2)dA\]

where \(R\) is the region \([0,1] \times [0,2]\).

Solution for Problem 1

We set up the double integral over the rectangular region \(R\) with limits \(x \in [0,1]\) and \(y \in [0,2]\):

\[\int_{0}^{1} \int_{0}^{2} (x^{2}y + xy + 2) \, dy \, dx\]

First, we integrate with respect to \(y\):

\[\int_{0}^{1} \left[ \frac{1}{2}x^2y^2 + \frac{1}{2}xy^2 + 2y \right]_{0}^{2} \, dx\]

Plugging in the limits for \(y\):

\[\int_{0}^{1} \left( \frac{1}{2}x^2(4) + \frac{1}{2}x(4) + 2(2) \right) \, dx = \int_{0}^{1} (2x^2 + 2x + 4) \, dx\]

Now, we integrate with respect to \(x\):

\[\left[ \frac{2}{3}x^3 + x^2 + 4x \right]_{0}^{1} = \frac{2}{3}(1) + (1)^2 + 4(1) = \frac{2}{3} + 1 + 4 = \frac{2}{3} + \frac{15}{3} = \frac{17}{3}\]

The value of the integral is \(\frac{17}{3}\).

Problem 2

Consider the solid enclosed between the graph of \(f(x,y)=y+1\) and the \(xy\)-plane lying above the triangular region whose vertices are \((-2,0)\), \((0,1)\) and \((2,0)\).

Visual Description: The region of integration is a triangle in the xy-plane. The base lies on the x-axis from x = -2 to x = 2. The peak of the triangle is at (0, 1). The left boundary is the line y = 1/2x + 1, and the right boundary is the line y = -1/2x + 1.

(a) (3 points) Write down a double integral that computes the volume of this solid.

(b) (3 points) Evaluate the above integral to compute the volume of the described solid.

Solution for Problem 2

Part (a): Setting up the Integral

The volume \(V\) is given by the double integral of the function over the region \(D\). The boundaries of the triangle are \(y = 0\) (bottom), \(y = \frac{1}{2}x + 1\) (left side), and \(y = -\frac{1}{2}x + 1\) (right side). It is easier to set this up as a Type II region where \(y\) goes from \(0\) to \(1\), and \(x\) goes from the left line to the right line.

Solving for \(x\): Left line is \(x = 2y - 2\); Right line is \(x = 2 - 2y\).

The integral is: \[V = \int_{0}^{1} \int_{2y-2}^{2-2y} (y+1) \, dx \, dy\]

Part (b): Evaluation

Integrate with respect to \(x\):

\[\int_{0}^{1} \left[ (y+1)x \right]_{2y-2}^{2-2y} \, dy = \int_{0}^{1} (y+1)((2-2y) - (2y-2)) \, dy\]

Simplify the integrand:

\[\int_{0}^{1} (y+1)(4-4y) \, dy = 4 \int_{0}^{1} (1+y)(1-y) \, dy = 4 \int_{0}^{1} (1 - y^2) \, dy\]

Final integration with respect to \(y\):

\[4 \left[ y - \frac{1}{3}y^3 \right]_{0}^{1} = 4(1 - \frac{1}{3}) = 4(\frac{2}{3}) = \frac{8}{3}\]

The volume of the solid is \(\frac{8}{3}\) cubic units.
