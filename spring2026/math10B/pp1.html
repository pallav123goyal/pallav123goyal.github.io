MATH 10B Practice Problems 1

1. Double and Triple Integrals

Problem 1: Evaluate Double Integrals

Evaluate the following double integrals:

a) \[\int_{0}^{4}\int_{y/2}^{2}e^{x^{2}}dxdy\]

b) \[\int_{0}^{1}\int_{y}^{1}e^{y/x}dxdy\]

c) \[\iint_{D}e^{x-y}dA\] where \(D\) is the triangular region enclosed by the points \((0,0)\), \((1, 3)\), and \((2, 2)\).

Problem 2: Compute Triple Integrals

Compute the following triple integrals:

a) \[\iiint_{T}2ye^{x}dV\] where \(T\) is the solid bounded by \(0 \le x \le 1\), \(0 \le x \le y\), and \(0 \le z \le x+y\).

b) \[\iiint_{T}y^{2}dV\] where \(T\) is the tetrahedron bounded by the plane \(2x+3y+z=6\) in the first octant.

c) \[\iiint_{T}x^{2}y^{2}z^{2}dV\] where \(T\) is the region bounded by \(z=y+1\), \(y+z=1\), \(x=0\), \(x=1\) and \(z=0\).

Problem 3: Compute Volume of Solids

Compute the volume of the following solids:

a) The solid enclosed by the paraboloids \(z=2-x^{2}-y^{2}\) and \(z=x^{2}+y^{2}\).

b) The solid enclosed by the surfaces \(x^{2}+y=1\) and \(z^{2}+y=1\).

c) The solid enclosed by the surfaces \(x^{2}+2y^{2}=2\), \(z=0\), and \(x+y+2z=2\).

Problem 4: Changing Order of Integration

Consider the triple integral: \[\int_{0}^{2}\int_{0}^{9-x^{2}}\int_{0}^{2-x}dzdydx\]

Express this as a triple integral such that the order of integration is:

  • \(dydxdz\)
  • \(dydzdx\)
  • \(dxdydz\)

2. Change of Variables

Problem 5: Change of Variables in Double Integral

Set up an integral to evaluate \[\iint_{D}\cos\left(\frac{x+y}{x-y}\right)dA\] over the region \(D\) enclosed by the points \((0,0)\), \((1, 1)\), \((2,0)\), and \((1,-1)\).

Problem 6: Enclosed Region Integral

Compute the integral \[\iint_{R}\frac{1}{x+y}dydx\] where the region \(R\) is enclosed by \(x=2\), \(x=5\), \(x+y=1\), and \(x+y=4\).

Problem 7: Parallelogram Area

Set up a single double integral to compute the area of the parallelogram with vertices \((0,0)\), \((2, 5)\), \((1,4)\), and \((3,9)\).

Problem 8: Variable Substitution

Compute the integral \[\iint_{R}(x+y)^{2}e^{x-y}dxdy\] where \(R\) is the region bounded by \(x+y=1\), \(x+y=4\), \(x-y=-1\), and \(x-y=1\).

Problem 9: Area Between Curves

Compute the area bound by the curves \(x^{2}-2xy+y^{2}+x+y=0\) and \(x+y+4=0\). Hint: Use \(u=x+y\), \(v=x-y\).

Problem 10: First Quadrant Area

Compute the area bound by the curves \(xy=1\), \(xy=4\), \(y=x\), and \(y=4x\) in the first quadrant.

Problem 11: First Quadrant Region

Evaluate the integral \[\iint_{D}xydA\] where \(D\) is the region in the first quadrant bounded by the curves \(x^{2}+y^{2}=4\), \(x^{2}+y^{2}=9\), \(x^{2}-y^{2}=1\), and \(x^{2}-y^{2}=4\).

3. Polar, Spherical, and Cylindrical Coordinates

Problem 12

Evaluate the following double integrals:

a) \[\int_{-1}^{1} \int_{-\sqrt{1-y^2}}^{\sqrt{1-y^2}} e^{-(x^2+y^2)} \, dx \, dy\].

b) \[\int_{1/2}^{1} \int_{0}^{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \, dy \, dx\].

Problem 13

Find the volume bounded below the \(xy\)-plane and above the surface \(x^2+y^2+z^6=1\).

Problem 14

Set up an integral to compute: \[\iint \frac{1}{(x^2+y^2)^2} \, dA,\] over the region enclosed between \(x^2+y^2=1\) and \(x+y=1\) in the first quadrant.

Problem 15

Find the area of the region enclosed by the curve \(r^2 = 4\cos(2\theta)\).

Problem 16

Evaluate the integral: \[\int_{-1}^{1} \int_{0}^{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \int_{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}^{1} \, dz \, dy \, dx\]

Problem 17

Integrate the function \(ze^{x^2+y^2}\) over the region enclosed by \(x^2+y^2 \le 4\) and \(2 \le z \le 3\).

Problem 18

Evaluate using cylindrical coordinates the integral: \[\iiint_{B} z \, dx \, dy \, dz\] where \(B\) is the region above the \(xy\)-plane, inside the cylinder \(x^2+y^2=1\), and below the cone \(z = \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\).

Problem 19

Find the volume enclosed above the plane \(z = 4+x\) and below the \(xy\)-plane inside the cylinder \(x^2+y^2 = 2x\).

Problem 20

Set up an integral to compute the volume of a sphere of radius \(r\) using:

  • Cartesian coordinates
  • Cylindrical coordinates
  • Spherical coordinates
Problem 21

Evaluate: \[\iiint_{R} \sin(x^2+y^2) \, dV\] where the region \(R\) is defined via: \(-1 \le x \le 1\), \(0 \le y \le \sqrt{1-x^2}\), and \(0 \le z \le 2\).

Problem 22

Compute the integral of the function \(f(x, y, z) = \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}e^{-(x^2+y^2+z^2)}\) over the unit ball centered at the origin.

Problem 23

Evaluate: \[\int _{0}^{2} \int_{0}^{\sqrt{4-y^2}} \int_{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}^{\sqrt{4-x^2-y^2}} (x^2+y^2+z^2) \, dz \, dx \, dy.\]

Problem 24

Set up an integral for the function \(f(x,y,z)=xyz\) over the region enclosed by \(x^2+y^2+z^2=9\) above the \(xy\)-plane.

Problem 25

Find the volume of the solid enclosed by the surfaces \(x^2 + y^2 = \frac{1}{5}z^2\) and \(z = 5 + \sqrt{5 - x^2 - y^2}\) above the \(xy\)-plane.
