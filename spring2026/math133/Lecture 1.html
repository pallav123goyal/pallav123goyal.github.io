Lecture 1: Introduction to Geometry

The word geometry is derived from the Greek words geo (meaning "rocks" or "earth") and metria (meaning "measurement").

Euclid: The Father of Geometry

Euclid was an ancient Greek geometer known for his foundational book, the Elements. He defined geometry as the study of shapes that can be constructed using only a straightedge and a compass.

Definition: A straightedge is a device used for drawing straight lines without any measurement markings.

Definition: A compass is a device used for drawing circles with a given center and radius.

Geometric Fundamentals

Using these tools, we can construct points, lines (including segments and rays), circles, arcs, and angles.

Definition: An angle is the region between two intersecting lines, segments, or rays.

Angles are measured in degrees or radians. A full circle consists of \(360^\circ\) or \(2\pi\) radians.

Recall: 1 radian is the angle where the arc length is equal to the radius. The circumference of a circle with radius \(r\) is given by \(C = 2\pi r\).

Euclid's Axioms

Euclid's goal was to start with basic "obviously true" statements called axioms and prove theorems using them.

Theorem: Euclid's Axioms

  1. It is possible to draw a unique straight line through any two points.
  2. It's always possible to extend a segment indefinitely.
  3. It's possible to draw a circle with any center and any radius.
  4. Any two right angles (\(90^\circ = \frac{\pi}{2}\) radians) are equal to each other.
  5. The parallel axiom.

Euclid used these to prove theorems: formally proved or logically deduced properties about objects based on known facts.

Proposition 1: Equilateral Triangle Construction

Definition: An equilateral triangle is a triangle with all sides equal.

Example: Constructing an Equilateral Triangle

Given: Points \(A\) and \(B\). Draw an equilateral triangle where one side is \(AB\).

Visual Description: The diagram shows two circles. Circle \(O_1\) is centered at \(A\) and passes through \(B\). Circle \(O_2\) is centered at \(B\) and passes through \(A\). The intersection of these circles is labeled point \(C\). A triangle is formed by connecting \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\).

Goal: Show that \(|AB| = |BC| = |CA|\).

Proof:

  1. \(AB\) and \(AC\) are both radii of circle \(O_1\), so \(|AB| = |AC|\).
  2. \(AB\) and \(BC\) are both radii of circle \(O_2\), so \(|AB| = |BC|\).
  3. Since both equal \(|AB|\), then \(|AB| = |BC| = |CA|\).

Therefore, triangle \(ABC\) is equilateral.

Definition: A regular polygon is a polygon that is equiangular (all angles are equal in measure) and equilateral (all sides have the same length).
